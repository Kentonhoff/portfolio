Recent Projects

Senior Capstone Course: User Testing a Client's Website

For our final senior capstone course, we conducted usability testing for the Inter-university Consortium for Political and Social Research (ICPSR) website, focusing on the browsing capabilties of the "Find Data" page and the search results page when users searched for data. ICPSR stores over 14,000 academic studies and datasets on their site, so we wanted to help users search that vast storage much more quickly without needing to consult an expert source like a librarian. We performed a competitive analysis on similar aggregator websites, sent out surveys to past visitors of ICPSR's website, interviewed existing users, and created user personas to help frame our approach. Then, we created some low fidelity wireframes in Balsamiq, conducted A/B testing to validate our redesign, and finalized a high-fidelity prototype in Adobe XD to help the client visualize some of the recommendations we thought could improve the ICPSR user experience.

Top Videogames and their Retention Value

This next project looked at the highest selling videogames of all time and the resale value of a mint condition physical copy of the game. I hoped to discover whether factors like the release date or the gaming platform contributed to game's worth. Used versions of a game are usually pretty cheap, but mint conditions of classics can sell for over $500 on auction sites like eBay.
